History of Nike

Nike was founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight. Rebranded in 1971, it introduced the Swoosh logo and "Just Do It" slogan in 1988. Key milestones include the first Nike shoe in 1972 and global expansion to over 190 countries with $44 billion in revenue by 2023.

Products

Shoes

Running (e.g., Nike Pegasus), Basketball (e.g., Air Jordan), Lifestyle (e.g., Air Force 1), and more.

Apparel and Accessories

Hoodies, shorts, jerseys, bags, and customizable options like Nike By You.

Innovations

Nike Air for cushioning, Flyknit for lightweight design, React Foam for energy return, and sustainability efforts like Move to Zero using recycled materials.

Endorsements

Partners with athletes like Michael Jordan, LeBron James, and Serena Williams, plus teams in NBA, NFL, and Olympics.

Other Features

Global presence with over 1,000 stores, sustainability initiatives, and apps like SNKRS for sneaker releases.
